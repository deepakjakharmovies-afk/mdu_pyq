Four hundred runs during India’s Under 19 World Cup 2020 campaign, youngest Indian to score a List A double ton at the age of 17. Yashasvi Jaiswal was bought by Rajasthan Royals for INR 2.40 Cr ahead of the IPL 2020. It was clear that RR looked at Yashasvi as a long term prospect and it was with that intention that he was retained ahead of the 15th season. In the 2021 season, Yashasvi had an impressive SR scoring 249 runs in 10 games from the 168 balls that he faced. He even got his maiden 50 in that season. He continued his good form in the 2022 season scoring 258 runs and was retained ahead of the 2023 auction. He went one step ahead in the 2023 season and smashed 625 runs at an average of 48.07 and a scintillating SR of 163.61. The year 2023 was special for the youngster as on the back of truckload of runs, he made his India debut in T20I and Test and went on to score 171 on his Test debut, against West Indies. Since then, the bright left-handed batter has not looked back and excelled at every opportunity at the highest level. He had a phenomenal 2024 year where he made debut for India in all three formats and was the second-highest run getter in Tests with 1478 runs in the calendar year. Being one of the five players retained by Rajasthan speaks volumes of his consistency and importance in the set-up. The Southpaw has been in blistering form and would form a crucial part of Rajasthan Royals' bid for the second title in IPL 2025.
