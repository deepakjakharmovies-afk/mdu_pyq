A rising star from Delhi’s cricketing circuit, Arya made a name for himself as a six-hitting machine while leading the Delhi U19 side. The stylish left-handed batter impressed with a gritty 71 on his first-class debut for Delhi in the 2024-25 Ranji Trophy against Tamil Nadu. He later announced his arrival in T20 cricket with a blistering 102 off 46 balls against Uttar Pradesh in the Syed Mushtaq Ali Trophy, showcasing his explosive stroke play. His talent was first spotted in local leagues, where he dominated with consistent big-hitting performances, racking up runs at a staggering strike rate and smashing record-breaking sixes. This caught the attention of IPL scouts, and ahead of the TATA IPL 2025, Punjab Kings secured him for INR 1 crore. Now set to feature on the biggest stage, Arya will have the opportunity to learn from stalwarts like captain Shreyas Iyer, coach Ricky Ponting, and Glenn Maxwell, an experience that will be invaluable in shaping his future.
